Methodology

Stablecoin attestation exposure counts three U.S. Treasury-related holdings:

Direct U.S. Treasuries Bills, notes, or bonds line-itemed on the issuer’s reserve report — not fund shares or other packaged vehicles.
U.S. Treasury repo Repurchase agreements collateralized by Treasuries.
Government money market funds Historically ~99% T-bills / repo.

Packaged / rehypothecated holdings (e.g. BUIDL inside a stablecoin’s reserves) are tracked separately and excluded from ex-rehypothecated totals. Standalone tokenized U.S. treasury products from RWA.xyz are included in Tokenized Assets alongside stablecoin ex-rehyp exposure.

Column definitions
Direct — owned U.S. Treasuries only (bills / notes / bonds on the attestation). Repo and government MMFs are separate columns.
Repo — U.S. Treasury repurchase agreements. These are typically overnight / 1–2 day repos and are treated as continuous U.S. Treasury holdings while rolled.
Gov MMF — government money market funds. Examples from attestations include Fidelity Government Money Market (CUSIP 31607A703, e.g. USD1) and government money-market fund holdings reported by issuers such as USDG and RLUSD.
Rehypothecated — tokenized U.S. treasury products (e.g. BUIDL) held on a stablecoin’s books. These are already counted in standalone tokenized treasury supplies, so including them here would double-count.
